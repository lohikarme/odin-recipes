cortado feat. niche zero & aergrind

Description

First sojourn into making espresso at home, I wanted to not get in over my head, but still have the required equipment for a minimum viable product.

Searching for secondhand equipment turned up nothing at the time, so I ordered the most inexpensive espresso machine and quality hand grinder available. This search lead me to the leaver arm espresso "machine" niche zero, and the made by knock aregrind.

The niche zero has treated me well and continues to make delicious espresso, though heat management is finicky.

The aregrind on the other hand has been abused. This device is intended for a courser grind destined for its namesake aeropress, though can easily grind for espresso. The markings on the device are a saviour for dialing in, though the accuracy and precision are not present at these finer grinds. Not only is it too sensitive for nailing espresso, the device will wander while grinding. This is ignoring the aluminum power I can find on my hands after use....To be fair, this is a great device that deserves to be retired with full honours before the wheels completely fall off.

Ingredients and Equipment

  • niche zero (includes: pressure gauge, resovoir, basket, tamper, showerscreen)
  • aergrind
  • 2x cup
  • 1000W microwave oven
  • kettle
  • scale
  • timer
  • espresso coffee beans
  • water
  • cream

Steps

  1. fill kettle and boil water
  2. weigh out 15g of coffee beans
  3. grind beans in aergrind (dial in and repeat as necessary)
  4. add ground beans to basket,tamp down, add showerscreen (do not use pressurized portafilter)
  5. heat cups and resevoir with boiling water
  6. microwave 70ml of cream for 23 seconds (I do not have steam and make do)
  7. drain cup and tare scale
  8. drain resevoir and attach to basket
  9. fill resevoir with boiling water and affix pressure gauge
  10. attach resevoir to niche zero
  11. prime at 3 bars for 3 seconds
  12. bring pressure to 9 bars (if dialed in correctly, I am usually aiming for 30g out over ~30 seconds)
  13. combine cream with espresso and sugar to taste
